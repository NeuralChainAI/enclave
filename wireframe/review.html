Limitation of liability — uncapped §11.2
The agreement places no cap on the vendor's aggregate liability. Your playbook requires a cap of 12 months' fees for vendor-side MSAs of this size. Leaving liability uncapped is the single highest-exposure deviation in this contract.

§3.4 — Term & Renewal

This Agreement shall remain in effect for an initial term of one (1) year and shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless either party gives written notice at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

§9.1 — Indemnification

Customer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Vendor from and against any and all third-party claims, damages, and expenses arising out of or relating to Customer's use of the Services.

§11.2 — Limitation of Liability

Except for breaches of confidentiality, no limitation shall apply to the aggregate liability of either party under this Agreement, and each party shall be liable for all direct and consequential damages.

§13.2 — Assignment

Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld.

§14.1 — Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict-of-laws provisions.

§15.3 — Notices

All notices shall be in writing and delivered by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the addresses set forth above.
